Why a New Benchmark?

Existing virtual try-on benchmarks evaluate models by masking the garment region on a person already wearing that garment, and asking the model to reconstruct it. This induces an input dependency bias — models learn to copy appearance from the reference image rather than the provided garment. No benchmark supports evaluating the practical scenario where the person wears a different garment, or adopts a different pose.

Evaluation Triplet

Each entry in our dataset is a triplet of (input person, target garment images, ground truth). The same person physically wears GB at a target pose to produce the ground truth — no synthetic compositing.

Pose Configurations

We evaluate across four pose configurations defined by the orientation of the target torso: front, left, right, and back. Pose labels are assigned based on where the majority of the body points — a person can have their head turned the other way and still fall into a given category. This reflects how poses naturally vary in the wild, rather than enforcing exact rotation angles.

Front → Front
Both input and target have the torso roughly facing the camera (within ±45°). Isolates garment transfer with minimal pose variation.
Front → Left
Target torso points left (roughly 45°–135°). Pose is assigned by majority body orientation — the head may still face the camera.
Front → Right
Target torso points right (roughly 45°–135°). Symmetric counterpart to Front → Left, with the same orientation conventions.
Front → Back
Target torso faces away (roughly 135°–225° from the camera). The head may still be visible while the body points back.

Evaluation Metrics

Because our dataset provides real ground truth images, we can apply full-reference metrics that are unavailable in existing VTON benchmarks.

LPIPS
Lower is better

Learned Perceptual Image Patch Similarity. Measures perceptual similarity using deep features; correlates better with human judgement than pixel metrics.

PSNR
Higher is better

Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio. A pixel-level fidelity measure. Provides a complementary signal to perceptual metrics.

SSIM
Higher is better

Structural Similarity Index. Captures luminance, contrast, and structure differences between generated and ground truth images.
